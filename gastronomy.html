The Flavors of Portugal

Savor a culinary landscape shaped by land and sea. From the iconic salted cod and rich olive oils to the world-famous pastéis de nata, Portuguese gastronomy is a heartfelt celebration of tradition, fresh ingredients, and bold flavors.

Pastel de Nata

The iconic Portuguese custard tart with flaky pastry and creamy filling, best enjoyed warm with a sprinkle of cinnamon.

Bacalhau

Discover the legendary salted cod prepared in over 365 different ways, one for each day of the year.

Francesinha

A legendary, hearty sandwich originating from Porto, layered with various meats, covered in melted cheese, and smothered in a rich, spiced tomato-beer sauce.

Sardinhas Assadas

Fresh, charcoal-grilled sardines that serve as the flavorful staple of Portuguese summer festivals, best enjoyed simply on a slice of rustic bread.

Arroz de Marisco

A vibrant, indulgent seafood rice dish prepared in a clay pot, overflowing with a bounty of fresh Atlantic shellfish and fragrant herbs.

Queijo da Serra

Portugal’s most famous traditional sheep’s milk cheese, prized for its unique, buttery texture and rich, slightly tangy flavor profile from the Serra da Estrela mountains.
