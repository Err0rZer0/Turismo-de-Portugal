A Land of Infinite Variety

From the snow-capped peaks of the Serra da Estrela to the volcanic craters of the Atlantic, Portugal’s geography is a spectacular mosaic.

The Continental Divide

The mighty Tagus River bisects the mainland. To the north lie lush, rugged mountains; to the south, the golden plains of the Alentejo.

Mount Pico

Rising 2,351 meters in the Azores, this volcano is Portugal's highest point.

The Veins of the Nation

The Douro and Tagus rivers carve spectacular gorges and sustain ancient vineyards.

The Atlantic Archipelagos

Far off the mainland, the Azores and Madeira offer subtropical forests and geothermal hot springs.

832km of Coastline

Bordering the Atlantic, featuring wild dunes in the west and limestone stacks in the Algarve.
